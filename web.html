About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an Islamic ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, India. Commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, the Taj Mahal is a stunning example of Mughal architecture. Its symmetrical design, intricate calligraphy, and beautifully landscaped gardens make it a globally recognized symbol of love and devotion.

Location

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is about 230 km (143 miles) from Delhi and 125 km (78 miles) from Gwalior. It is situated on the southern bank of the Yamuna River, easily accessible via road and rail networks.

History

The construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was largely completed in 1648, with additional structures finished in subsequent years. The mausoleum was built using white marble from Makrana, Rajasthan, and features exquisite carvings, calligraphy, and semi-precious stone inlays. The complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and expansive gardens. Over 20,000 artisans and laborers were involved in its construction under the guidance of chief architect Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 and continues to be one of the most visited monuments in the world.
